Privacy Policy

Your data stays yours.

Last updated: 17 June 2026

Testwright Pro (the “Extension”) is a browser extension that helps QA engineers and developers generate test cases, extract Playwright selectors, and audit web forms. This policy explains exactly what data the Extension handles and where it goes.

In short: Testwright Pro does not have any servers. The developer does not collect, receive, store, see, sell, or share your data. There is no tracking, no analytics, no advertising, and no user account. Everything happens locally in your browser, except requests you explicitly send to the AI provider you choose.

01 Data the Extension handles

The Extension works with the following data, entirely on your device:

  • Your AI provider API key. If you enter an API key for a cloud provider (such as Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, Groq, NVIDIA, DeepSeek, Qwen, or Kimi) or configure a local model server (such as Ollama or LM Studio), the key and server settings are stored locally in your browser using chrome.storage.local.
  • Requirement text and page content you submit. When you generate test cases, pick elements, extract locators, auto-fill forms, or run the form tester, the relevant text or page content is processed locally and—only for AI generation—sent to the AI endpoint you have configured.
  • Your preferences. Settings such as theme, output language, selected provider, and a local activity log are stored locally in your browser.

02 Where your data goes

  • To the developer: never. The Extension has no backend. No data is ever transmitted to the developer or to any third party operated by the developer.
  • To your chosen AI provider: only when you act. When you click a button that generates content, your requirement text or page content is sent directly from your browser to the AI endpoint you selected in Settings. If you use a local model (e.g. Ollama on localhost), the data never leaves your machine. If you use a cloud provider, the data is sent to that provider and is subject to their privacy policy and terms.

You are responsible for choosing your AI provider and for reviewing that provider’s privacy practices. Testwright Pro simply forwards your request to the endpoint you configured.

03 On-page access

Features such as Pick Element, Extract Locators, Auto-Fill, Scan, and the Form Tester read or interact with the page in your active tab. This happens only when you explicitly click a button for that action. The Extension does not run in the background, does not monitor your browsing, and does not read pages you are not actively testing. The “all sites” host permission exists because you—not the developer—choose which website to test; the Extension has no predetermined target site.

04 Data storage and retention

All settings and keys are stored locally via chrome.storage.local and remain on your device until you change them or remove the Extension. Uninstalling the Extension removes this locally stored data. The developer cannot access this data at any time.

05 Data the Extension does NOT do

  • No analytics or usage tracking.
  • No advertising or ad networks.
  • No selling or sharing of data with third parties.
  • No use of data for credit, lending, or any purpose unrelated to the Extension’s function.
  • No user accounts, sign-in, or personal-profile collection.

06 Children’s privacy

The Extension is a professional developer tool and is not directed to children. It does not knowingly handle data from children under 13.

07 Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the “Last updated” date above will be revised. Material changes will be reflected in the Chrome Web Store listing.
